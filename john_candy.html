Early Life

Candy was born on October 31, 1950, in Newmarket, Ontario. The son of Sidney James Candy and Evangeline (n√©e Aker) Candy, he was brought up in a working-class Roman Catholic family. Candy's father was of English and Scottish descent, while his mother was of Polish and Ukrainian descent. His father, Sidney, died of a heart attack at age 35 in 1955 when John was only five years old. As a child, he lived in a small house on 217 Woodville Avenue in East York, Ontario.

Candy studied at Neil McNeil Catholic High School in Toronto. He dreamed of becoming a football player long before acting was a consideration, but an injury to his knee prevented him from playing. He later enrolled in Centennial College to study journalism, and then went to McMaster University. It was in college that he first tried theater, and soon developed skill and love for the art.
